New in v1.3.0

Study Mode is here.

Save multiple timestamps, add notes to each one, and turn any YouTube video into a proper research session.

What's new

Multiple timestamps per video

Save as many moments as you need in a single video. No more overwriting your last save.

Notes on every timestamp

Add a personal note to each saved moment so you remember exactly why it mattered.

Switch modes freely

Toggle between Resume Mode and Study Mode anytime. Your data is always kept safe.

Made for moments like these

Learning

Watching a tutorial or lecture

Mark every concept you want to revisit and note what confused you so you can come back with focus.

Research

Taking notes on a long video

Save key moments with your own commentary — like a notebook that lives inside the extension.

Reference

Building a personal knowledge base

Collect the best moments from multiple videos with context, ready to export to Markdown.

Casual

Just want to resume a video?

Study Mode is optional. Resume Mode still works exactly as before — one tap, right back where you left off.

How to get started

1

Open the extension popup

Click the Save & Resume icon in your browser toolbar to open the popup.

2

Tap your profile picture

Click the profile avatar in the top right corner to open the account menu.

3

Turn on Study Mode

Toggle Study Mode on. All your video cards will switch to Study Mode immediately. You can also turn it on per video using the three-dot menu on any card.

4

Save timestamps on YouTube

Go to any YouTube video. Press the Save Timestamp button or Alt+S. A modal will appear where you can add a note right as you save.

5

Expand your card to see everything

Back in the popup, click Expand on any Study Mode card to see all your timestamps and notes in one place. Click any note to edit it inline.

Your existing timestamps are safe. Nothing was deleted or changed by this update. Resume Mode still works exactly as before. Study Mode is a completely optional layer on top of what you already use.

Try Study Mode on YouTube

Open any video, switch on "Study Mode", press Option/Alt+S, and add your first note.
